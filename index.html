The Opportunity

Chicago's South and West Sides represent one of the largest unmet needs in urban mental health. As institutions have withdrawn, communities have been left without accessible, free, peer-led support.

NAMI Chicago is uniquely positioned to fill that gap, not as a clinical provider, but as something no other organization in the city offers at scale.

What We Would Explore Together

The Questions That Matter

These are not recommendations. They are the questions a strategic visioning process should wrestle with, the kinds of conversations that produce plans worth following.

1

Growth Model

You have tripled in five years. Is the current model built to sustain $10M+ in annual revenue, or does the infrastructure need to evolve first? The FY2024 deficit and audit finding suggest the organization may have outgrown some of its operational systems.

2

Revenue Resilience

At 67.5% government-funded, with federal grants proving unreliable, what does a resilient revenue mix look like? Your 11.7 months of reserves give you the rare luxury of building new revenue streams without survival pressure. How do you use that window?

3

Niche vs. Expansion

Your strength is peer support, education, and advocacy. As you grow, how do you expand reach without drifting into clinical territory where Thresholds, Trilogy, and Heartland Alliance operate with budgets 5-18x your size?

4

Community Trust

South and West Side expansion is the right strategic move. But what does authentic, sustained, community-partnered presence look like in practice? These communities have experienced decades of institutional withdrawal. Authentic presence requires co-investment, local hiring, sustained presence, and genuine partnership with organizations already doing the work.

5

Leadership Moment

Matt Davison inherits an organization 3.6x the size it was when the current strategic plan was written. How do you set the next chapter up for success when the organization is 3.6x the size it was when the current plan was written? The next strategic plan will be his plan. Getting it right matters more than getting it fast.
